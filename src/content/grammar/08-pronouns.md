## Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns in Greek refer to the speaker (first person), the listener (second person), and someone else (third person). Unlike some languages, Greek pronouns don't always distinguish gender.

### The Personal Pronouns

**First Person (I, we):**

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | ἐγώ (I) | ἡμεῖς (we) |
| Genitive | ἐμοῦ (of me) | ἡμῶν (of us) |
| Dative | ἐμοί (to me) | ἡμῖν (to us) |
| Accusative | ἐμέ (me) | ἡμᾶς (us) |

**Second Person (you):**

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | σύ (you) | ὑμεῖς (you [pl.]) |
| Genitive | σοῦ (of you) | ὑμῶν (of you [pl.]) |
| Dative | σοί (to you) | ὑμῖν (to you [pl.]) |
| Accusative | σέ (you) | ὑμᾶς (you [pl.]) |

**Third Person (he, she, it, they):**

Greek uses the definite article (ὁ, ἡ, τό) as a third-person pronoun:

| Case | Masculine Sing. | Feminine Sing. | Neuter Sing. | Masculine Pl. | Feminine Pl. | Neuter Pl. |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | ὁ | ἡ | τό | οἱ | αἱ | τά |
| Genitive | τοῦ | τῆς | τοῦ | τῶν | τῶν | τῶν |
| Dative | τῷ | τῇ | τῷ | τοῖς | ταῖς | τοῖς |
| Accusative | τόν | τήν | τό | τούς | τάς | τά |

### Demonstrative Pronouns

**This/These: οὗτος, αὕτη, τοῦτο**
- Nominative singular masculine: οὗτος
- Nominative singular feminine: αὕτη
- Nominative singular neuter: τοῦτο

**That/Those: ἐκεῖνος, ἐκείνη, ἐκεῖνο**
- Nominative singular masculine: ἐκεῖνος
- Nominative singular feminine: ἐκείνη
- Nominative singular neuter: ἐκεῖνο

### The Reflexive Pronoun (self)

**αὐτός** is used both as a demonstrative adjective ("same") and reflexive ("self").

- ὁ αὐτός = the same
- ἑαυτοῦ = of myself/yourself/himself (reflexive genitive)
- ἑαυτῷ = to myself/yourself/himself (reflexive dative)

### Examples from John 3

**Verse 3:** "ἀπεκρίθη Ἰησοῦς καὶ εἶπεν αὐτῷ"
- αὐτῷ = "to him" (dative masculine singular)
- Refers back to Nicodemus

**Verse 16:** "ἠγάπησεν ὁ θεὸς τὸν κόσμον"
- ὁ = nominative masculine singular (he = God)
- τὸν = accusative masculine singular (him/it = world)

### Relative Pronoun (who, which, that)

**ὅς, ἥ, ὅ** — agrees with its antecedent in gender and number, but takes its case from its function in the relative clause.

- τὸν ἄνθρωπον ὃν εἶδες = the man whom you saw
  - ἄνθρωπον = accusative (direct object of εἶδες)
  - ὃν = neuter accusative relative (matches the grammatical function, not the antecedent)

### Key Observations

1. **Greek distinguishes singular and plural** in you and we, unlike modern English
2. **Gender is marked** in second and third person singular (not always)
3. **The article functions as a pronoun** in the third person
4. **Word order matters less** in Greek — pronouns mark relationships clearly through case

### Common Pronoun Combinations

- ἐμοί = to me (1st person dative singular)
- σοί = to you (2nd person dative singular)
- αὐτῷ = to him/her/it (3rd person dative singular)
- ἡμῖν = to us (1st person dative plural)
- ὑμῖν = to you (2nd person dative plural)
- αὐτοῖς = to them (3rd person dative plural)

Personal pronouns are essential for understanding relationships between people and events in NT narratives!
