## The Greek Article

Greek has **only one article**: ὁ, ἡ, τό (the). There is no indefinite article ("a" or "an" in English).

### Function of the Article

The definite article serves several functions:

1. **Definiteness** — Makes a noun definite ("the word" rather than "a word")
   - ὁ λόγος — the word
   - λόγος — a word (without article)

2. **Gender, Number, and Case Marker** — The article agrees with its noun in ALL three categories
   - ὁ λόγος — nominative singular masculine
   - τὴν λόγον — accusative singular masculine

3. **Substantive Function** — Can be used alone to represent a noun
   - ὁ Θεός — The God (understood "the one who is God")
   - ὁ καλός — The beautiful one (understood as a person)

### The Article Paradigm

See the paradigm table above for the complete article declension across:
- **3 genders**: masculine, feminine, neuter
- **2 numbers**: singular and plural
- **4 cases**: nominative, genitive, dative, accusative

### Key Observations

**Nominative forms are distinctive:**
- Masculine singular: ὁ
- Feminine singular: ἡ
- Neuter singular: τό
- Masculine plural: οἱ
- Feminine plural: αἱ
- Neuter plural: τά

**Genitive and dative plural are identical across all genders:**
- Genitive plural: τῶν (all genders)
- Dative plural: τοῖς (all genders)

### Examples from John 1:1

**ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος**
- "In the beginning was the word"
- ὁ = nominative singular masculine article
- λόγος = nominative singular masculine noun

**καὶ ὁ λόγος ἦν πρὸς τὸν Θεόν**
- "And the word was with God"
- τὸν = accusative singular neuter article (neuter because Θεός is being treated as neuter here)
- Θεόν = accusative singular neuter noun

**καὶ ὁ λόγος ἦν ὁ Θεός**
- "And the word was God"
- ὁ λόγος = nominative singular (the subject "word")
- ὁ Θεός = nominative singular (predicate nominative, meaning "God")

### When to Use the Article

1. **With specific, known nouns**
   - ὁ ἀποστόλος — the apostle (a specific apostle already mentioned)

2. **With adjectives used as nouns**
   - ὁ ἀγαθός — the good one (a righteous person)
   - ἡ ἀγάπη — the love (abstract concept)

3. **With proper nouns in certain contexts**
   - ὁ Ἰησοῦς — Jesus (sometimes omitted for emphasis)
   - ἡ Ἰουδαία — Judea

### When NOT to Use the Article

1. **With indefinite nouns**
   - λόγος — a word

2. **With predicate nominatives (descriptive)**
   - οὐχ ἕν ἐστιν — "it is not one" (one thing)

3. **In certain fixed expressions and titles**

The article is **the most frequently used word in the Greek New Testament**, appearing over 19,000 times! Mastering its use is essential.
