## Adjectives in Greek

Greek adjectives **must agree** with their nouns in **gender, number, and case**. This agreement rule is fundamental to Greek grammar.

### The Agreement Rule

An adjective **must match its noun** in:
1. **Gender** (masculine, feminine, neuter)
2. **Number** (singular, plural)
3. **Case** (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative)

### Examples of Agreement

**From John 1:4**
- τὸ φῶς τῆς ζωῆς (the light of life)
  - τὸ φῶς = neuter singular nominative
  - τῆς ζωῆς = feminine singular genitive (ζωή is feminine)

**From Matthew 3:2**
- ἐγγίζει ἡ βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν (The kingdom of heaven is near)
  - ἡ βασιλεία = feminine singular nominative
  - τῶν οὐρανῶν = masculine plural genitive
  - Here "heavens" is plural, so the adjective/noun must agree

### The Three Classes of Adjectives

**Class 1: First-and-Second Declension** (Three endings)
- Nominative masculine singular: -ος
- Nominative feminine singular: -η or -α
- Nominative neuter singular: -ον
- Example: **καλός** (beautiful) — καλὸς, καλή, καλόν

**Class 2: First-and-Second Declension** (Two endings)
- Masculine and feminine share the same form
- Example: **ἁγιος** (holy) — ἅγιος (m./f.), ἅγιον (n.)

**Class 3: Third Declension** (Variable forms)
- Often have diverse nominative endings
- Example: **πᾶς** (all) — πᾶς, πᾶσα, πᾶν

### Comparative Adjectives

Greek forms comparatives and superlatives with specific endings:

**Comparative (-τερος/-τέρα/-τερον):**
- σοφός → σοφώτερος (wiser)
- μεγάλη → μεγαλοτέρα (greater)

**Superlative (-τατος):**
- σοφός → σοφώτατος (wisest)
- μεγάλη → μεγαλοτάτη (greatest)

Some irregular forms:
- ἀγαθός → ἀμείνων (better)
- κακός → χείρων (worse)

### Adjective Position and Meaning

The position of an adjective relative to its article can change meaning:

**Attributive Position** (adjective between article and noun):
- ὁ ἀγαθὸς λόγος = the good word (the word is good)

**Predicate Position** (adjective after the noun or without article):
- ὁ λόγος ἀγαθός = the word is good
- λόγος ἀγαθός = (a) good word

### Practice with Matthew 5:8

**μακάριοι οἱ καθαροὶ τῇ καρδίᾳ**
(Blessed are the pure in heart)

- μακάριοι = nominative plural masculine (adjective = blessed)
- οἱ = nominative plural masculine article
- καθαροὶ = nominative plural masculine adjective (pure)
- τῇ καρδίᾳ = dative singular feminine (heart)

Notice the agreement: μακάριοι and καθαροὶ both match the plural masculine implied by the article.

### Key Points

1. **Adjectives always agree** with their nouns in gender, number, and case
2. **Word order isn't as strict** as in English — look for agreement markers
3. **Predicate adjectives** can appear after the noun without the article
4. **Third declension adjectives** are irregular but very common (πᾶς, μέγας, πολύς)

Agreement is your key to understanding relationships between words in Greek!
