## First Declension Overview

The first declension includes mostly **feminine nouns** that end in **-ή**, **-α**, or (rarely) **-ᾱ** in the nominative singular.

### Characteristics

- **Mostly feminine**, though a few masculine forms exist (like ὁ μαθητής — the disciple)
- **Endings are regular and predictable**
- **Most common feminine noun pattern in the NT**
- **Genitive plural ending is always -ῶν**

### Pattern: -ή Nouns (ἀγάπη — love)

The -ή ending nouns are the most regular. See the paradigm table for ἀγάπη above.

**Key features:**
- Nominative singular: -ή
- Nominative plural: -αι
- Genitive plural: -ῶν
- The stem remains constant: ἀγάπ-

### Masculine -ης Nouns (ὁ μαθητής — the disciple)

Though rare in first declension, some masculine nouns follow this pattern:

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | ὁ μαθητής | οἱ μαθηταί |
| Genitive | τοῦ μαθητοῦ | τῶν μαθητῶν |
| Dative | τῷ μαθητῇ | τοῖς μαθηταῖς |
| Accusative | τὸν μαθητήν | τοὺς μαθητάς |

**Note:** These can take the definite article masculine (ὁ), showing they are masculine.

### Common First Declension Words

| Greek | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| ἀγάπη | love | 1 John 4:7 — ἡ ἀγάπη (the love) |
| δόξα | glory | John 1:14 — τὴν δόξαν αὐτοῦ (his glory) |
| δοῦλη | female slave | Luke 1:38 — ἡ δοῦλη κυρίου (the handmaid of the Lord) |
| κώμη | village | Mark 11:2 — εἰς τὴν κώμην (into the village) |
| ἡμέρα | day | Mark 16:2 — τῇ ἡμέρᾳ (on the day) |
| μαθήτρια | female disciple | Acts 21:10 — μαθήτριες (female disciples) |
| οἰκία | house | Matthew 2:11 — εἰς τὴν οἰκίαν (into the house) |
| ψυχή | soul/life | John 10:11 — τὴν ψυχήν αὐτοῦ (his life) |

### Accent Rules for First Declension

Most first declension nouns have persistent accents:
- If the accent falls on the ultima (final syllable) in the nominative, it remains there in oblique cases
- **Example:** ἀγάπη → ἀγάπης → ἀγάπῃ

### Practical Example: Matthew 26:26 (The Last Supper)

**λαβὼν τὸν ἄρτον, εὐλόγησεν καὶ κλάσας ἔδωκεν τοῖς μαθηταῖς**

- τοῖς μαθηταῖς = dative plural of μαθητής (to the disciples)
  - Article: τοῖς (dative plural, all genders)
  - Noun: μαθηταῖς (masculine first declension dative plural)

### Key Takeaway

The first declension is **regular and predictable**. Once you master ἀγάπη, you can decline virtually any -ή noun. The main variations are:
1. Some -α nouns (less common)
2. Masculine -ης forms (like μαθητής)

Practice declining multiple words to internalize the pattern!
